About Me

A story of how a cramped after-school classroom, an app built during COVID, and a few great internships turned into a long-term bet on finance, ML, and security.

Opening Balance Grade 11 · First Line of Code

I wrote my first line of code in grade 11, sitting in a cramped classroom after school. I had no idea what I was doing — but I was completely absorbed by the feeling that I could build anything with a few lines of logic. That spark pushed me into the Technovation challenge, where my team and I built VolunTime, a mobile app designed to help students find volunteer opportunities more easily during COVID. We centralized listings, added fast filters, and focused on reducing the frustration of scattered information. To our surprise, VolunTime made it to the Technovation quarter-finals, and for the first time, I saw how software could genuinely improve people’s lives.

Reallocation Choosing Carleton

As I started applying to universities, Carleton stood out immediately — the tight-knit engineering community, the quieter campus, and the feeling that I could actually grow there. After becoming a Raven, I dove headfirst into exploring what kind of engineer I wanted to be.

New Asset Class BlackBerry IVY & Beyond

My first internship at BlackBerry IVY opened the door to embedded systems, data pipelines, and model optimization. From there, each experience layered on something new: building backend systems, working with distributed services, writing security-focused tools, and learning how to build software that is reliable at scale.

Current Position Finance × ML × Security

Today, I’m a Software Engineering student at Carleton University with 4+ years of experience building backend systems, security-oriented tooling, and data-driven pipelines. I love working at the intersection of finance, machine learning, and security — turning complex data into meaningful, trustworthy systems that people can rely on.

Future Growth Still Chasing the Spark

And I’m still chasing that same feeling I had in grade 11: the excitement of building something that matters.
